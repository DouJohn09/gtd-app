questions

What people ask first.

When is Cleartable launching? +
Soft launch on r/gtd within weeks, broader release shortly after. Join the waitlist to be notified first — the first 30 buyers can lock in the refundable $30/yr founder price before Pro settles at $36/yr.
Does it work on mobile? +
Yes — Cleartable is a web app that works on any device with a browser. A PWA (installable home-screen app, offline support) is shipping before public launch, so no app store wait.
Do I need to know GTD to use it? +
Not at all. The structure (Inbox, Next Actions, Projects, Someday/Maybe) is intuitive even without studying David Allen's book. GTD practitioners get a system built around their methodology; everyone else gets a calm task manager that just works.
Is my data private? +
Yes. Your tasks are stored privately, never sold, and never used to train external AI models. AI features call our providers (Groq and OpenAI) on demand only — per their API policies, your input isn't used for training. You can export everything anytime. See our Privacy Policy.
Can I cancel or get a refund? +
Anytime. Cancel from Settings and you keep Pro until your period ends — no charge after that. New subscriptions are refundable for 14 days, and the founder pre-sale is fully refundable within its launch window. Details in our Refund Policy.
Can I export my data? +
Always. Full JSON backup and Todoist-compatible CSV export are built in. Your data is yours — leave anytime, no lock-in.
Why not just use Todoist or Things? +
Todoist treats GTD as an afterthought (no defer dates, no review mode). Things is Apple-only and has no AI. OmniFocus is Apple-only and costs $10/month. Cleartable is cross-platform, GTD-native, and AI-powered — and Pro comes in under Todoist Pro.
