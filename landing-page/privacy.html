Privacy Policy

Last updated: 13 June 2026

This policy explains what personal data Cleartable collects, why, who we share it with, and the rights you have over it. Cleartable is operated by an individual entrepreneur established in the Czech Republic, and this policy is written to comply with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Who is responsible for your data

The data controller is Jan Bambas, an individual entrepreneur (OSVČ) registered in the Czech Republic, IČO 14136074, registered at [registered address — fill in]. For any privacy question or to exercise your rights, contact us at support@cleartable.app.

2. What data we collect

Account data

  • If you sign in with Google: your email address, name, and profile picture, provided by Google OAuth. We never receive or store your Google password.
  • If you sign up with email: your email address and a password, which we store only as a salted one-way hash.

Content you create

  • Tasks, projects, notes, contexts, habits, custom lists, and the metadata you attach to them (due dates, energy levels, etc.). This is your data — we process it only to provide the service.

Google Calendar (optional)

  • Only if you explicitly connect it: OAuth access/refresh tokens, calendar events we read to display alongside your tasks, and events we create in a dedicated "Cleartable" calendar. You can disconnect at any time, which revokes and deletes these tokens.

Payment data

  • Payments are processed by Paddle, our Merchant of Record. We never receive or store your card details. We receive a customer/subscription identifier, your plan, and its status so we can unlock paid features.

Technical data

  • An authentication token (JWT) and preference settings stored in your browser's local storage. We do not use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

3. How we use your data

  • To provide and operate the app (store and sync your tasks, run the GTD workflow).
  • To process your text with AI features when you use them (see section 5).
  • To manage your subscription and unlock paid features.
  • To send you essential account and launch communications. Marketing emails are sent only with your consent (e.g. waitlist signup) and you can unsubscribe at any time.

4. Legal bases for processing (GDPR Art. 6)

  • Performance of a contract — providing the app and your account.
  • Consent — connecting Google Calendar, and marketing emails. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legitimate interests — keeping the service secure and functioning.
  • Legal obligation — tax and accounting records (handled via our Merchant of Record).

5. AI processing

When you use an AI feature (Smart Capture, Process Inbox, Daily Focus, Weekly Review, and similar), the relevant text is sent to a third-party AI provider on demand to generate the result. Only the text needed for that feature is sent. AI suggestions are processed in real time and are not used by us to build advertising profiles.

  • Groq — primary provider for fast capture and objective processing. Per Groq's policy, API inputs are not used to train their models.
  • OpenAI — used as a fallback and for advisory features. Per OpenAI's API data-usage policy, data submitted via the API is not used to train their models.

7. International transfers

Some of our providers process data outside the EEA (e.g. in the United States). Where that happens, transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and, where applicable, the providers' certification under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework.

8. How long we keep it

We keep your account and content for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account or ask us to delete your data, we remove it from our active systems, with backups cycling out shortly after. Records we are legally required to keep (e.g. transaction records, held by our Merchant of Record) are retained for the period the law requires.

9. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you;
  • Correct inaccurate data;
  • Delete your data ("right to be forgotten");
  • Restrict or object to processing;
  • Data portability — and note Cleartable has built-in JSON and CSV export, so you can take your data at any time;
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any of these, email support@cleartable.app. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Czech supervisory authority, the Office for Personal Data Protection (Úřad pro ochranu osobních údajů, uoou.gov.cz).

10. Security

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS). Passwords, where used, are stored only as salted hashes. We restrict access to production data to what is necessary to operate the service. No online service can guarantee absolute security, but we take reasonable measures appropriate to the data we hold.

11. Children

Cleartable is not intended for anyone under 16, and we do not knowingly collect data from children.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the product evolves. Material changes will be reflected in the "last updated" date above, and significant changes affecting your rights will be communicated by email where appropriate.

13. Contact

Questions about privacy? Email support@cleartable.app.
